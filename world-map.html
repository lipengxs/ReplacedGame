REPLACED Phoenix-City Map Guide

REPLACED is not an open-world checklist game. Its map is a cinematic route through Phoenix-City and the areas around it, where platforming, combat, visual storytelling, and R.E.A.C.H.'s identity all share the same space. The best route notes read like a field journal: what the room teaches, what can kill you, what can be missed, and whether the segment is worth replaying later.

Route Types

  • Story corridors: move slowly and read background detail.
  • Combat arenas: learn enemy spacing before pushing forward.
  • Platforming hazards: watch foreground/background movement.
  • Environmental puzzle rooms: scan light, doors, switches, and path shape.

Phoenix-City Notes

The official premise frames Phoenix-City as a corrupt corporate stronghold in an alternate 1980s America. The city is not just scenery; it explains why R.E.A.C.H. is hunted, used, and forced to learn what being human means.

Replay Cleanup

Post-launch quality-of-life features make cleanup easier. Use level select for missed collectibles, dialogue skip only after a first read, and a letterbox toggle if visual framing affects your route practice.

What To Track

Keep a simple route log: scene name, combat room, platform hazard, collectible clue, and whether the segment is safe to replay without spoilers. Start with the beginner guide.

Route Log Template

Use this structure when turning a play session into a walkthrough. It keeps the page practical without spoiling the story too early.

  • Scene label: Use a neutral name such as "neon alley chase" or "factory platform room" until the official chapter name is confirmed.
  • Main threat: Combat timing, platforming hazard, puzzle layout, or story-only scene.
  • Missable notes: Visual clue, collectible, optional dialogue, or environmental detail.
  • Replay reason: Practice, cleanup, screenshot capture, or lore review.

Exploration Rhythm

Do not sprint through every screen. REPLACED is built around the pressure between cinematic pacing and playable action. In quiet rooms, read signs, silhouettes, background movement, and lighting direction. In fight rooms, stop looking for secrets until the arena is safe. In platforming rooms, watch the whole frame before jumping; the game often uses visual framing to tell you where the safe path is.
